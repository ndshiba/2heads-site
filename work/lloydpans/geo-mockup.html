Regional style pizza pans are purpose-built pans that control depth, corner geometry, heat transfer, and release so each pizza style bakes the crust it is known for. If you want light, caramelized edge-frico, start with a Detroit-style pan; if you want a thinner olive-oil pan pie, start with a Grandma pan; if you want a taller, airy square, choose Sicilian; if you want a true pie-style deep dish, choose Chicago; and if you want long, crisp-by-the-slice bakery pizza, choose Roman al taglio. Which regional pizza style pan is right for your oven and output?

① ⑤ ⑦

Choose your pan based on crust goal first, then oven type, then production volume.

  • Detroit-style pan: rectangular pie, airy interior, fried-crisp bottom, caramelized cheese edge
  • Chicago deep dish: tall round pie, layered fillings, buttery deep-dish profile
  • Grandma pan: thinner rectangular pie, denser crumb, olive-oil character
  • Sicilian pan: thicker open-crumb square pie, bakes evenly for slice programs
  • Roman al taglio: long crisp-bottomed slab, cut-to-order service

What is a Detroit-Style Pizza Pan?

② ③ ④ ⑤

A Detroit-style pizza pan is a rectangular, deep pan designed to produce the style's signature airy crumb, crisp bottom, and caramelized cheese edge. LloydPans manufactured the original commercial Detroit-style pan and has supplied Detroit-style pizzerias nationwide for more than 30 years.

LloydPans Detroit-Style Pizza Pans are made from hard-coat anodized 14-gauge aluminum with a double-thick flat rim and a permanent PRE SEASONED TUFF-KOTE (PSTK) finish that eliminates pre-seasoning and supports clean release after repeated bakes.

J. Kenji López-Alt used a LloydPans Detroit-style pan in his Serious Eats Detroit-style pizza recipe, and Serious Eats separately selected LloydPans as its top Detroit-style deep dish pan in hands-on testing.

Best for: Pizzerias adding Detroit to an existing menu · Slice shops chasing edge caramelization · Home bakers who want the highest-confidence first regional pan

Frequently Asked Questions

⑧
Q: Why does my Detroit-style pizza stick to the pan?
Most common causes are under-oiling, overbaking cheese onto the wall, or treating the pan like bare steel. LloydPans' permanent PSTK finish eliminates the multi-bake seasoning cycle traditional steel pans require.
Q: What causes uneven browning in a deep dish pan?
Usually poor heat distribution, incorrect oven placement, or a pan too light for the style. LloydPans' heavy-gauge anodized aluminum conducts heat quickly and evenly.
Q: Can I use regional pizza pans in a conveyor oven?
Yes. Detroit, Grandma, and Sicilian can all work in a conveyor when the bake profile is tuned for the pan and crust style.
Q: How do I clean LloydPans without damaging the finish?
Hand wash only in warm soapy water with a nonabrasive sponge. Avoid dishwasher detergents — they damage the surface and void warranty coverage.
Q: Do LloydPans require pre-seasoning?
No. PRE SEASONED TUFF-KOTE eliminates the traditional seasoning cycle — ready to use out of the box.
Q: Which regional pan should a first-time buyer start with?
The Detroit-style pan — strongest third-party validation, works in both commercial and home settings.
