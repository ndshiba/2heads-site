Overview

May was a solid month for organic performance. Website traffic reached 1,418 sessions, driven primarily by Direct, Organic Search, and Organic Social. Real organic search traffic — the visitors who found the site through Google — grew compared to May 2025 in both volume and quality, and overall site engagement improved meaningfully.

There is one metric that looks alarming on the surface: Google Search impressions dropped roughly 70% year over year. The data tells a more nuanced story — that number does not reflect a loss of real traffic. It reflects the removal of low-quality, buried rankings that were never driving business anyway. That story is covered in full below.

The chart below shows the divergence plainly. Impressions collapsed. Clicks held flat. These two lines tell opposite stories — and that gap is the whole point.

May 2025 vs. May 2026  ·  Impressions on left axis (bars), Clicks on right axis (line)

Between mid-2024 and early 2025, Google updated its algorithm to demote location-stacked service area pages — pages built to capture search traffic by combining a service keyword with a long list of city names. Colao & Peter had dozens of these:

/custom-pool-design-renovations-sterling-washington-dc-leesburg-great-falls-mclean-arlington-va/

These pages were ranking on pages 4–9 of Google for searches like “landscape design,” “pool installation,” and “custom deck builder.” They generated enormous impression counts — but at positions 40–90, essentially no one sees them. Click-through rates were effectively zero. Of the 163,094 impressions in May 2025, over 100,000 came from those buried rankings and contributed fewer than a dozen actual clicks.

Google removed those rankings. 24 of the 25 top impression-driving pages from May 2025 are no longer appearing in search results. The impressions disappeared with them.

The site is appearing less often in search results — but where it does appear, it ranks better and gets clicked more. Average position improved from 43.9 to 22.6. Click-through rate tripled. Actual clicks grew. The impression decline is not a business problem.

Recommended Next Steps
1

“Luxury pool contractor” — dedicated content opportunity

Semrush shows the site ranking #4 for “luxury pool contractor” (390 monthly searches). Position 4 is firmly in clickable territory, but the ranking is likely spread across multiple pages rather than anchored to one strong page. A focused page — portfolio content, process detail, specifics — could consolidate that ranking and push it into the top 3.

2

Service page content depth — medium term

The pages that were penalized were thin on substance. The pages that survived are stronger. Continuing to build genuine depth — project case studies, process explanations, specific materials and techniques — protects existing rankings and creates the foundation for recovering broader keyword visibility over time.
