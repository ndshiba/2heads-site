Methodology

Data source: GA4 Data API, property 387610456, date range April 1 – May 31, 2026.

The signal used is the generate_lead GA4 event, filtered to sessions where the event fired on a page path containing /contact. The sessions metric counts distinct GA4 sessions meeting that condition — not individual event firings.

Landing page is the first page a user hit in their session before eventually reaching the contact page. Source and medium reflect GA4's last-non-direct session attribution model.

One row containing colaopeter1stg.wpenginepowered.com as session source was excluded as staging traffic.

Limitations
  • Submit clicks, not completions. The generate_lead tag fires when the Submit button is clicked — it does not confirm successful form completion. These 61 sessions include people who clicked Submit but may have abandoned due to validation errors or other friction. The number of actual submissions during this period is unknown.
  • Event multi-firing. The generate_lead tag fires on all Submit buttons site-wide, not exclusively the contact form. The sessions metric mitigates this (a session is counted once regardless of how many times the event fires within it), but sessions touching /careers/ or other pages with Submit buttons are not excluded.
  • No form_submitted event exists yet. A dedicated GA4 event tied to Elementor's confirmed form completion signal (elementorFormSubmit) is not yet implemented. Until it is, Submit-click sessions are the closest available proxy.
  • Small sample. 61 sessions across two months. Percentages are directional, not statistically reliable at this scale.
  • Staging data — May 2026. A GTM fix blocking staging hostnames was published June 11, 2026. May data may contain a small amount of staging-originated sessions that could not be fully isolated at the session level.
