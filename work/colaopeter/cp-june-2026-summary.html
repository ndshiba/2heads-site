🏡 Colao & Peter — June 2026 Performance Summary

Prepared July 10, 2026  ·  GA4  ·  Google Search Console  ·  Semrush

Overview

June was the strongest traffic month of 2026 so far. Total sessions reached 2,041 — up 44% from May — driven by organic search holding steady, organic social continuing to grow, and new paid Meta activity adding incremental reach. The overall site engagement rate dipped to 30.8%, but that's a mix-shift effect: the social and paid channels pulling the average down carry low engagement by design. The underlying quality of search and referral traffic improved.

The standout result of the month isn't in the traffic numbers — it's the pool renovations article. Published at the end of May, Luxury Pool Renovations: Reimagining What You Already Have generated 8 conversions from just 30 sessions in June. A 26.7% conversion rate. That's the highest of any page on the site, and it's a direct result of putting detailed, specific content in front of people already deep in the consideration process.

Paid Social (Meta — Instagram and Facebook ads) is new this month at 281 sessions. Engagement is low at 6%, which is normal for top-of-funnel paid social. The goal at this stage is reach and brand exposure, not immediate conversion. It's worth watching over the next few months whether paid social visitors return via direct and eventually convert.

AI assistant traffic (ChatGPT, Gemini) brought 14 sessions at 73% engagement — a small but meaningfully high-quality signal. Visitors arriving from AI tools are already informed and intentional. That number will grow as AI-referred traffic increases across the industry.

The chart below shows the same pattern seen in May: impressions collapsed year over year while actual clicks grew. Both lines are telling the truth — they just describe different things.

Jun 2025 vs. Jun 2026  ·  Impressions on left axis (bars), Clicks on right axis (line)

As covered in the May report, Google's 2024–2025 algorithm updates demoted the location-stacked service pages that dominated C&P's impression counts. Pages like this one:

/custom-pool-design-renovations-sterling-washington-dc-leesburg-great-falls-mclean-arlington-va/

These pages were ranking at positions 40–90, generating enormous impression counts with effectively zero clicks. Of June 2025's 158,907 impressions, the vast majority came from those buried rankings. Google removed them, and the impressions disappeared with them.

What's left is a cleaner, more accurate picture of the site's real search presence. Average position improved from 42.5 to 20.9 — a 22-spot jump. Click-through rate nearly quadrupled. Actual clicks grew. The trajectory is in the right direction.

The impression decline is not a business problem. The site is appearing less often in search results — but where it does appear, it ranks better and gets clicked more. That pattern held in May and it held again in June.

Recommended Next Steps
1

Amplify the pool renovations article

The article converted at 26.7% in its first full month — 8 leads from 30 sessions. That's not luck; it's the right content reaching visitors already in consideration mode. The next step is driving more of the right visitors to it: internal links from the pool service pages, a featured placement on the homepage, and a quick GSC review to see which queries are already finding it organically.

2

Second long-form article — pool houses or outdoor living

The pool renovations article proved the format works. The next content investment should follow the same pattern: a specific topic that Colao & Peter clients are actively researching, written with the depth and specifics that only an experienced contractor can provide. Pool houses, pergolas, or outdoor kitchens are all candidates with meaningful search volume and clear alignment to C&P's portfolio.

3

Evaluate Paid Social as data accumulates

281 sessions from Meta ads in the first month. Too early to draw conclusions on ROI — one month of data isn't enough to see return-visit or delayed conversion patterns. By August there should be enough signal to assess whether the audience targeting is reaching the right Northern Virginia market and whether any of those visitors come back and convert.
